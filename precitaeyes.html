Mural Arts

We redesigned the website of Precita Eyes, a mural arts non-profit in San Fransisco. Although we had all seen their murals around the city, the original website portrayed the originization much smaller and less impactful then they are.

Our initial understanding

Precita Eyes is a community of muralists.
By improving their site, more locals may become interested in murals

Our research findings

Precita Eyes is a community around murals.
By improving their site, more locals may understand where they are in San Fransiscos heiritage



Our target persona, Hannah


A Tech worker who recently moved to San Francisco, Hannah wants to mitigate her impact on local communities, and participate in local culture, but doesn’t know how.

She’s looking for local communicty non-profits to contribute to. She wants to join the community, not just donate money.

Hannah's needs Precita Eyes offerings
Wants to prevent local culture from being displaced by new residents Focus on murlas as a means toward preserving the culture of the Mission
Wants to know a non-profit is solving problems that local communities care about Founded 40 years ago and run by long time mission residents
Wants to understand the local culture Offers tours that explain the Mission’s culture and history
Wants to participate in the community Offers tours that explain the Mission’s culture and history

Precita eyes is a community that can introduce new arrivals to San Fransisco bay culture and history. Hoever, their original web layout does not communicate their mission but puts the burdon of research on visitors. It also fails to present their art desireably. This poor disign breaks the connection point between potential members and a thriving community.

    With this data, we created a problem statement.

    The Problem San Francisco transplants feel disconnected from the local community, and worry about their role in its gentrification and cultural dilution.
More on San Fransisco Background

    San Fransisco is a very deverse places, many cultures and backrounds combine to create a unique city. As many middle-class Americans move to the SF bay, they take the homes of low income residents, diluting local culture.

    This mural arts community celibrates and visualizes The Bay's culture; transplants that want to understand their new homes can partisipate through art.




User Testing

We conducted user tested to better understand what was or wasn't working with their website.

User testing insight #1

Website colors distract from their art.

User testing insight #2

Information architecture is needlessly complex.

User testing insight #3

Forms are inconsistent and ask for all user information at once.


      Based on our user testing we made four guidlines that would define a sucessful redesign:

  1. Made their mission clear
  2. Showcased their art well
  3. Communicate the credibility they have
  4. Made visitors want to paint The City

Layout

We remove nested navigation, tradiing fewer levels for more scrolling. Long and nested dropdown menus confused users and undermined their confidence. Next we expiramented with different ways to present the art. This S-shaped layout breaks information and artwork into bite sized chucks for new visitors to take in and quickly understanding what each page offers (these wireframes have been remade after settling on this design).





Look and feel
